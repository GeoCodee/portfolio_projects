EMAIL

What is email?

Email, short for "electronic mail", allows users to send and receive messages to and from anyone with an email address, anywhere in the world.


Features of email

  • Automatic reply to messages
  • Auto-forward and redirection of messages
  • Facility to send copies of a message to many people
  • Automatic filing and retrieval of messages
  • Addresses can be stored in an address book and retrieved instantly
  • Notification if a message cannot be delivered
  • Emails are automatically date and time stamped
  • Signatures can be attached
  • Files, graphics or sound can be sent as attachments, often in compressed formats
  • Webmail and mobile email can be used to receive and send messages while on the move

Using Email

Sending email
  1. Open the email client, or if using webmail open a browser and enter the webmail address, eg http://www.yahoo.com and log in.
  2. Choose 'New' or 'Compose'.
  3. Enter the intended recipient(s) email address(es) in the 'To' field.
  4. Write a suitable subject in the 'Subject' field.
  5. Write the message in the space below the Subject field.
  6. Click 'Send' to send the email.
  7. There's the option to add an attachment, eg a photo.

Receiving email
  1. Email clients and webmail periodically check for new emails. A manual check can be performed by clicking 'Send and Receive' in an email client or by refreshing the page when using webmail.
  2. Email clients download attachments automatically but webmail users must choose to download attachments.

Advantages of Email

  • Emails are delivered extremely fast when compared to traditional post.
  • Emails can be sent anytime (24 hours a day, 365 days a year)
  • Webmail means emails can be sent and received from any computer, anywhere in the world, that has an internet connection.
  • Cheap - when using broadband, each email sent is effectively free. Dial-up users are charged at local call rates but it only takes a few seconds (for conventional email, eg text only) to send an email.
  • Emails can be sent to one person or several people.
